The Need to Know's of Ball

  1. Offense
    • Your part in the offense is to help the coach's gameplan. Lean into your strengths.
    • Nothing is better for the team than a good playmaker. Learn how to dribble, pass, and take care of the ball.
    • Shooters shoot. Develop a strong shooting mechanic and practice your scoring ability regularly.
  2. Defense
    • They've always wondered whether technique or effort is more important for defense. The answer is both.
    • Always have your eyes on the ball, and the man you are guarding. Ball and man always. (BAMA)
    • When the shot goes up, put your body on your man and seal him on the outside. Go and chase that rebound.
